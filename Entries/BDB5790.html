Biblical Hebrew

I. עֶגְלָה noun feminine heifer; — absolute ׳ ע Gen 15:9 +; construct עֶגְלַת Isa 7:21 +; suffix עֶגְלָתִי Judg 14:18 plural construct עֶגְלוֺת Hos 10:5 (but see below); — heifer, Isa 7:21, used in ploughing Judg 14:18 (figurative), threshing Jer 50:11 (דָשָׁא ׳ ע, in simile, see דּוּשׁ , read perhaps דָשָׁה), figurative of stateliness etc. Jer 46:20 מְלֻמָּדָה ׳ ע Hos 10:11 a trained heifer (simile of Ephraim); used for sacrifice 1Sam 16:2 (עֶגְלַת בָּקָר ); cut in two for ratifying covenant Gen 15:9 ( J ; מְשֻׁלֶּשֶׁת ׳ ע; compare עֵגֶל Jer 34:18; Jer 34:19); in cleansing city from blood-guiltiness Deut 21:3 (בָּקָר ׳ ע), Deut 21:4 Deut 21:4 Deut 21:6 once of calves worshipped in Northern Israel Hos 10:5 (where read probably עֵגֶל, so 𝔊 We Now GASm, compare Che, see also the following suffixes masculine singular).
