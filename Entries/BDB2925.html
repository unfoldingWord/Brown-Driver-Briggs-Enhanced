חִטָּה noun feminine wheat (Late Hebrew id., Aramaic חִנְטִין, חִיטְּתָא, ; Arabic ); — absolute ׳ח Exod 9:32 + 6 t.; plural חִטִּים Gen 30:14 + 20 t.; חִטִּין Ezek 4:9 construct חִטֵּי Ezek 27:17 — wheat, singular chiefly in poetry, of growing wheat Exod 9:32 (E), Deut 8:8; Job 31:40; Joel 1:11 sown Isa 28:25 food-product Deut 32:14 (׳חֵלֶב כִּלְיוֺת ח kidney-fat of wheat, i.e. the choicest, see חֵלֶב), ׳חֵלֶב ח Ps 81:17 (compare Ps 147:14 below); elsewhere plural; wheat as sown Jer 12:13 wheat-harvest ׳קְצִיר ח Gen 30:14 (J), Exod 34:22 (JE), Judg 15:1; 1Sam 6:13; 1Sam 12:17; Ruth 2:23, + 2Sam 24:15 where insert according to ᵐ5 We Dr; wheat threshed Judg 6:11; Judg 1:21 compare v1Chr 20:23 measured 1Kgs 5:25 = 2Chr 2:9 (חטים מַכּוֺת, read מַכֹּלֶת ׳ח as 1Kgs 5:25 = wheat for food Th Be Ke after Vrss), v.1Kgs 4:14; 1Kgs 27:5; Ezek 45:13 stored (with barley, oil and honey) Jer 41:8 for food 2Sam 17:28, חֵלֶב חִטִּים Ps 147:14 (compare Ps 81:14 above); לֹקְחֵי חִטִּים 2Sam 4:6 (read ׳סֹקְלָה ח ᵐ5 We Dr); ׳סֹלֶת ח fine wheaten flour Exod 29:2 (P); Ezek 4:9 (חִטִּין, Aramaic plural) mixed with barley, beans, lentils, etc., and made into bread.
