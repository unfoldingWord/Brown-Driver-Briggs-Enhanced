Biblical Hebrew

לִשְׁכָּה noun feminine room, chamber, hall, cell, lishka (Late Hebrew id. ᵑ7 לִשְׁכְּתָא; compare also נִשְׁכָּה; on the lishka see NowArchaeology ii. 37, 52, 79; BoGeogr.I, xvii. 438 (ed. Leusd.) compare Greek λέσχη, compare RsSemitic i. 236 (2nd ed. 254) and elsewhere); — ׳ ל Ezek 40:38 +; construct לִשְׁכַּת 2Kgs 23:11 +; with ה locative לִשְׁכָּ֑תָה 1Sam 9:22 plural לְשָׁכוֺת Ezek 40:17 +; construct לִשְׁכוֺת Neh 10:38 +; —

1.
a. of room connected with a sanctuary (בָּמָה), where sacrificial meal was eaten 1Sam 9:22, insert also probably 1Sam 1:18 ( 𝔊 We Dr Klo Bu).
b. connected with Solomon's temple, where wine offered to Rechabites Jer 35:2; Jer 35:4 compare Jer 35:4 Jer 35:4 one where prophecy read Jer 36:10 (both these apparently open, compare Gie Jer 35:2 ); compare לִשְׁכַּת נְתַןמֶֿלֶךְ הַסָּרִיס ֗֗֗ בַּפַּרְוָרִים 2Kgs 23:11 used of Levites' cells 1Chr 9:33 = store-rooms 1Chr 9:26 (|| אֹצָרוֺת), 1Chr 23:28; 1Chr 28:12; 2Chr 31:11 .
c. in Ezekiel's temple, cells for priests, singers, etc. Ezek 40:17 (twice in verse); Ezek 40:38 ; Ezek 40:44 ; Ezek 40:45 ; Ezek 40:46 + Ezek 41:10 (strike out Co Bthl), Ezek 42:1; Ezek 42:4; Ezek 42:7; Ezek 42:8; Ezek 42:11 + Ezek 42:12 (strike out Co Bthl); in three tiers, compare הָעֶלְיוֺנֹת ׳ הַלְּ Ezek 42:5 (see v Ezek 42:3), ( Ezek 45:5 read עָרִים לָשֶׁבֶת 𝔊 Sm Co SgfrKauAT Bthl); הַקֹּדֶשׁ ׳ ל for sacred uses Ezek 42:13 (3 t. in verse); Ezek 44:19; Ezek 46:19.
d. in second temple, as store-rooms Ezra 8:29; Neh 10:38; Neh 10:39 (|| בֵּית הָאוֺצָר), v.Neh 10:40 used by individuals Ezra 10:6; Neh 13:5 ( גְּדוֺלָה ׳ ל), Neh 13:8, in general Neh 13:4 Neh 13:9.
2. of scribe's room in royal palace Jer 36:12; Jer 36:20; Jer 36:21.
