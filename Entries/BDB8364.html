שָׁאוֺן noun masculine Hos 10:14 roar (of waters, etc.), din, crash, uproar; — absolute ׳ שׁ Hos 10:14 +; construct שְׁאוֺן Isa 13:4 +; suffix שְׁאוֺנָהּ Isa 5:14 —

1. roar of water (waves), Isa 17:12 (|| הָמָה), simile of noise of invading host, so v Isa 17:13, but strike out Du CheHpt Marti, as doublet; roar of this host vIsa 17:12 (|| הָמוֺן), compare שְׁאוֺן קוֺלָם Jer 51:55 of waves also Ps 65:8 (twice in verse); of din, or crash of battle Amos 2:2; Hos 10:14; Ps 74:23 ׳ בְּנֵי שׁ Jer 48:45 (i. e. warriors); of battle in which ׳ י beats down his foes Jer 25:31, compare Isa 66:6 of gathering hosts Isa 13:4 זָרִים Isa 25:3 ( Du Marti, plausibly, גְּאוֺן זְדִים, as Isa 13:11); read ׳ שׁ also probably (for שַׁאֲנָן , q. v.) 2Kgs 19:28 = Isa 37:29 in mocking appell. of Pharaoh (Necho), Jer 46:17 ׳ פּ ( ᵐ5 ᵑ6 ᵑ9 ) קִרְאוּ שֵׁם call ye the name of Phoenician a crash.
2. uproar of revellers Isa 5:14; Isa 24:8. — ׳ בּוֺר שׁ Ps 40:3, pit of roaring (of waters? figurative, compare מַעֲמַקֵּימַֿיִם Ps 69:3, where also || יָוִן); Thes and others of destruction (see √ 2), but against usage of שָׁאוֺן; > Du proposes בּוֺר שָׁוְא (compare נָפַת שָׁוְא Isa 30:28 ).
