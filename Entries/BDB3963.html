Biblical Hebrew

כִּיֹּר, כִּיּוֺר noun masculine 1Kgs 7:38 pot, basin (Late Hebrew כִּיּוֺר wash-basin, so Aramaic כִּיּוֺרָא) — absolute כִּיֹּר 1Kgs 7:30 + 8 t.; כִּיּוֺר 1Sam 2:14 + 5 t.; construct כִּיּוֺר Exod 30:18 + 2 t.; plural absolute כִּיֹּרוֺת 1Kgs 7:40 (but see below), 2Chr 4:14 כְּיֹּרֹת 1Kgs 7:43 כִּיּוֺרִים 2Chr 4:6 construct כְּיֹּרוֺת 1Kgs 7:38 —

1. pot for cooking 1Sam 2:14 (|| דּוּד, קַלַּחַת, פָּרוּר ).
2. fire-pot כִּיּוֺר אֵשׁ Zech 12:6 (|| לַפִּיד אֵשׁ; figurative of chiefs of Judah).
3. basin of bronze for washing, laver; set on a bronze support
a. before tabernacle Exod 30:18; Exod 30:28; Exod 31:9; Exod 35:16; Exod 38:8; Exod 39:39; Exod 40:7; Exod 40:11; Exod 40:30; Lev 8:11 (all P ).
b. 10 in number, 5 at each front corner of temple 1Kgs 7:30; 1Kgs 7:38 (3 t. in verse); 1Kgs 7:43 = 2Chr 4:14; 2Chr 4:6 (1Kgs 7:40 read הַסִּירוֺת as 1Kgs 7:45 and || 2Chr 4:11; 2Chr 4:16, so Hebrew Manuscripts 𝔊 ᵑ9 Th Ke Sta Klo and others)
4. platform or stage of bronze (probably round, bowl-like in shape) on which, according to Chronicles, Solomon stood and kneeled 2Chr 6:13.
