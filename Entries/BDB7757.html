Biblical Hebrew

II. רָגַע verb be at rest, repose (probably = Arabic return, properly return to rest, after wanderings, etc.); —

Niph`al Imperative 2 feminine singular Jer 47:6 (of sword) be gathered into thy scabbard, הֵרָֽגְעִי וָדֹ֫מִּי repose, and be still.
Hiph`il
a. transitive give rest to; — Infinitive construct Jer 31:2 הָלוֺךְ לְהַרְגִּיעוֺ I will go to give him (Israel) rest , Jer 50:34 לְמַעַן הִרְגִּיעַ אתהֿאדץ (on ׳ הִ, see Ges«GKC:53l»§ 53l Dr Deut 7:24 ; read probably ׳ הַ); Isa 51:4 ומשׁפטי לאור עמים אַרְגִּיעַ usually I will cause my judgment (religion) to repose as, etc. (i.e. I will establish it; compare שִׂים Isa 42:4); but metaphor strange: hence Bachm Che Marti (joining to Isa 42:5) צִדְקִי (< אֲקָרֵב Isa 46:13) אַרְגִּיעַ אַקְרִיב in a moment (I. רָגַע Hiph`il) will I bring near, etc., Du הִרְגִּיעַ קָרֵב, Oort Ry Kit בְּרֶנַע קָרֵב (compare ᵐ5 ἐγγίζει ταχύ).
b. intransitive rest, repose, Deut 28:65 ובגוים ההם לא תַרְגִּיעַ, Isa 34:14 שָׁם הִרְגִּ֫יעָה לִילִית. So Eccl 36:31 .
