BDB1964 | BIBLICAL HEBREW | BDB1966

דֳּמִי noun [masculine] cessation, pause, quiet, rest; ׳ ד Isa 62:6 + 2 t.; construct דְּמִי Isa 38:10 (but see below); —

1. quiet, in phrase לָכֶם ׳ אַלדֿ Isa 62:6 keep not quiet (let there be no quiet to you); in prayer to God לָךְ ׳ אַלדֿ Ps 83:2 keep not quiet (inactive; || חרשׂ, שׁקט); compare לוֺ ׳ וְאַלתִּֿתְּנוּ ד Isa 62:7 and give no rest to him (׳ י).
2. in phrase בִּדְמִי יָמַי, apparently quiet, peacefulness, even tenour, of my days (so De Or SS ); others, as Hi Ew Che Di, pause, resting-time, i.e. noon-day (compare ᵑ6 ᵑ9 , & height ᵐ5 ), figurative of middle life, but usage dubious; KloSK 1884, 157 cessation, pause, of natural end of life, Hezekiah's natural expectation, in, contrast with the speedy death implied in vb; but parallelism of Isa 62:11a is ag. this; Brd emends בְּרֻם after ᵐ5 ; Klol.c. suggests בְּתֹם or כְּתֹם as possible, though not necessary; — but view stated first is on the whole best.
