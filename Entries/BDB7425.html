BDB7425 [H7017]

Biblical Hebrew

קֵינִי adjective [of a people] of II. קַיִן 1 (q. v.), ׳ חֶבֶר הַקּ (dwelling in North Israel), ὁ Κειναῖος Judg 4:11; Judg 4:17 (twice in verse); Judg 5:24 (against WMM Jen, who refers ׳ ק here to city Kîn near Megiddo, mentioned by Egyptian, see especially Bu Now on Judg 4:11); read probably also ׳ חֹבָב הַקּ Judg 1:16 (for ׳ בְּנֵי ק; so Bu GFM; 𝔊 οἱ υιὁὶ Ιοθορ τοῦ Κειναίου); elsewhere as name of a people collective ׳ הַקּ settled among Amalekites, Num 24:21 ( J E ; see II. קַיִן 1), 1Sam 15:6 — = קֵינִי 1Sam 15:6 (We Bu Kit קַיִן, HPS הַקֵּינִי); — ׳ הַקּ also 1Sam 27:10; 1Sam 30:29 ( B 𝔊 L Κενεζ(ε)Ι =קְנִזִּי A Κηνελ, Κειναῖος); Gen 15:19 (list of peoples; R ); = הַקִּינִים 1Chr 2:55 (Κ (ε)ιναῖοι), related to Rechabites.
