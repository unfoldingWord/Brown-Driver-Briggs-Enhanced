BDB9112 [H8414]

Biblical Hebrew

תֹּ֫הוּ noun masculine 1Sam 12:21 (AlbrZAW xvi (1896), 112) formlessness, confusion, unreality, emptiness (primary meaning difficult to seize; Vrss usually Κενόν, οὐδέν, μάταιον, inane, vacuum, vanum ; compare LagOr. ii. 60; BN 144); —

1. formlessness, of primaeval earth Gen 1:2 ( P ), of land reduced to primaeval chaos Jer 4:23 (both + וָבֹהוּ and voidness), Isa 34:11 ׳ קַותֿֿ (|| אַבְנֵי בֹהוּ), Isa 45:18 בְרָאָהּ ׳ לֹא ת (|| לָשֶׁבֶת יְצָרָתּ); Isa 24:10 ׳ קִרְיַתאתּ city of chaos (of ruined city); = nothingness, empty space, Job 26:7 תֹּלֶה אֶרֶץ ׳ עַלתּֿ; of empty, trackless waste Deut 32:10 (|| מִדְבָּר), Job 6:18; Job 12:24 = Ps 107:40.
2. figurative of what is empty, unreal, as idols 1Sam 12:21 (collective: אֲשֶׁר ׳ אַחֲרֵי הַתּ לֹא יוֺעִילוּ), 1Sam 12:21; Isa 41:29 נִסְכֵּיהֶם ׳ רוּחַ וָת, Isa 44:9 (of idol-makers), groundless arguments or considerations, Isa 29:21 צַדִּיק ׳ וַיַּטּוּ בַתּ, Isa 59:4 moral unreality or falsehood ׳ בָּטוֺחַ עַלאתּ (|| וְדַבֶּרשָֿׁוְא); = a thing of nought (compare Eccl 41:10 מתהו אל תהו), Isa 40:17 (|| אַיִן אֶפֶס), Isa 40:23 עָשָׂה ׳ שֹׁפְטֵי אֶרֶץ כַּתּ (|| לְאַיִן), worthlessness Isa 49:19 וְהֶבֶל כֹּחִי כִלֵּיתִי ׳ לְת (|| לְרִיק יָגַעְתִּי); as adverb accusative Isa 45:19 I said not, תֹּהוּ בַקְּשׁוּנִי seek me emptily, to no purpose. compare Isa 29:13 𝔊 וְתֹהוּ for וַתְּהִי.
