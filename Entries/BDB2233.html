[הַרְמוֺן] noun masculine meaning dubious; text perhaps corrupt; only Amos 4:3 וְהִשְׁלַכְתֶּנָֿה הַהַרְמ֫וֺנָה and ye shall cast them (your אחרית , posterity, AE; Hi RV cast [yourselves], but read rather with Vrss as passive ׳ וְהָשְׁל shall be cast) into Harmon; if text be sound, some locality must be meant, though the nature of the allusion is lost ( AV , into the palace, treats הרמון improbable as = ארמון ). ᵑ7 ᵑ6 have mountain(s) of Armenia (הַר מוֺנָה), Symm Armenia, compare LagGes. Abh. 172, HoffmZAW iii. 1882, 102, of exile; Hi-St for ׳ ה read הֲדַדְרִמּוֺנָה to Hadadrimmon, proper name, of a location in plain of Jezreel, so Gunning; ᵐ5 εἰς τὸ ὄρος τὸ Ῥομμαν, whence Ew ingeniously ההר רמונה and ye shall cast Rimmonah (name of idol, supposed feminine of Rimmon 2Kgs 5:18) to the mountains.
