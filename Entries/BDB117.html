Biblical Hebrew

אֱדוֺם ( אֱדֹם Ezek 25:14)

proper name, masculine 1. Edom (name of a god ? see StaG. 1, 121 RSSem 43; see n.pr. עבדאדם; but BaeRel 10 thinks dialect variant reading of אָדָם, בני אדום = בְנֵי אָדָם) = Esau, elder son of Isaac Gen 25:30 ( J ) (where etymology = red, compare Gen 25:25 ( E ?) & below אַדְמוֺנִי Gen 36:1; Gen 36:8; Gen 36:19 ( P ).
2. collective (masculine but feminine Mal 1:4) Edomites, Idumeans as descendants of Esau Gen 36:9; Gen 36:43 ( P ); also 1Sam 14:47 + 31 t. + 2Sam 8:13 (for ᵑ0 ארם; 𝔊 ᵑ6 see 1Chr 18:12; Ps 60:2 , We Dr); perhaps also Ps 60:12 ( 𝔊 ᵑ6 1Chr 18:11 ᵑ0 ארם but see We Dr); also 2Chr 20:2 (see Berthau); ׳ א = king of Edom Num 20:18; Num 20:20; Num 20:21 in poetry ׳ בְּנֵי א Ps 137:7 ׳ בַּת א Lam 4:21; Lam 4:22
3. land of Edom, Idumaea (f. Ezek 32:29 ; Ezek 35:15 and Ezek 36:5) south & southeast of Palestine Gen 36:32; Gen 36:43 + 32 t. (including שְׂדֵה ׳ א Gen 32:4 ׳ אֶרֶץ א Gen 36:16; Gen 36:17; Gen 36:21; Gen 36:31; Num 20:23; Num 21:4; Num 33:37 +); — uncertain weather 2 or 3 are Exod 15:15; Num 20:14; Num 24:18 + 9 t. (chiefly in ׳ מֶלֶח א etc.)
