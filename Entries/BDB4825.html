BDB4825 [H4838]

Biblical Hebrew

I. [מָרַק] verb scour, polish (Late Hebrew id.; Aramaic מְרַק; compare Arabic scrape off wool from a skin); —

Qal Imperative מִרְקוּ הָֽרְמָחִים Jer 46:4 polish the lances! Passive participle מָרוּק 2Chr 4:16 polished, of bronze utensils of temple (= מְמוֺרָט 1Kgs 7:45).
Hiph`il Imperfect 3 feminine singular חַמְרִיק only Prov 20:30 Kt , figurative, in clause בְּרָ֑ע ׳ חַבֻּרוֺת פֶּצַע ת blows that cut in, cleanse away evil (compare De Now Wild VB ); Qr תַּמְרוּק i.e. (are) a cleaning. ( 𝔊 συναντᾷ κακοῖς, whence Gr תַּקְדִּים, Frankenb. תִּקְרֶינָה , but improbable).
Pu`al Perfect וּמֹרַק consecutive Lev 6:21 be well scoured, of bronze vessel (|| וְשֻׁטַּף בַּמָּ֑יִם).
