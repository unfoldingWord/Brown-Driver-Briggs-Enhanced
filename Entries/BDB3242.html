חִתִּי adjective and name, of a people Hittite(s) (Egyptian –-tá, –etá W Max MüllAsien u. Europa, 319 ff.; Assyrian –atti , SchrKG 192 ff. COT on Gen 10:19 DlPa, 269 ff.; Tel el-Amarna –atti, –atta BezTel el Amarn. Brit. Mus. 150) — masculine always with article הַחִתִּי Gen 23:10; Exod 3:8 +; feminine חִתִּית Ezek 16:3; Ezek 16:45 masculine plural הַחִתִּים Josh 1:4 + 3 t.; feminine plural חִתִּיֹּת 1Kgs 11:1 —

1. adjective, of seller of Machpelah to Abraham, עֶפְרוֺן הַחִתִּי Gen 23:10; Gen 49:29; Gen 49:30; Gen 50:13 עֶפְרֹן בֶּןצֹֿחַר ׳ הַח Gen 25:9 (all P ); of fathers of Esau's wives בְּאֵרִי ׳ הַח Gen 26:34 ׳ אֵילוֺן הַח vGen 26:34; Gen 36:2 (all P ); also of warriors of David, ׳ אֲחִימֶלֶךְ הַח 1Sam 26:6, and especially ׳ אוּרִיָּה הַח 2Sam 11:3; 2Sam 11:6; 2Sam 11:17; 2Sam 11:21; 2Sam 11:24; 2Sam 12:9; 2Sam 12:10; 2Sam 23:39; 1Kgs 15:5; 1Chr 11:41 feminine singular = substantive, only of (religious) ancestry of Jerusalem Ezek 16:3 thy father was the Amorite, and thy mother was a Hittite woman חִתִּית, similarly vEzek 16:45 feminine plural = substantive of Solomon's foreign wives, חִתִּיֹּת 1Kgs 11:1 Hittite women (+ מוֺאֲבִיּוֺת, עַמּוֺנִיּוֺת, אֲדֹמִיֹּת, צֵדְנִיֹּת).
2. noun usually
a. collective הַחִתִּי the Hittites: in lists of Canaanitish peoples Gen 15:20 ( J E ), Exod 3:8 ; Exod 3:11 (both J ), Exod 13:5 ; Exod 23:23 ; Exod 23:28 ; Exod 33:2 ; Exod 34:11; Num 13:29; Josh 3:10 (all J E ), Deut 7:1 ; Deut 20:17; Josh 9:1; Josh 12:8; Josh 24:11 (all D ); read also for הַחִוִּי Judg 3:3 (so We Mey BuUrg 350); in Josh 11:3 ( D ) strike out הַחִתִּי We Mey Bul.c., and read then הַחִתִּי for הַחִוִּי vb (so ᵐ5 , not ᵐ5 L ); further 1Kgs 9:20 = 2Chr 8:7; Ezra 9:1 ; Neh 9:8.
b. plural ׃הַחִתִּים כֹּל אֶרֶץ הַחִתִּים Josh 1:4 ( D ) nearly = land of Canaanites; ׳ ארץ הַח specifically of northern home of Hittites Judg 1:26 so also 2Sam 24:6 where read אֶרֶץ הַחִתִּים קֵ֫דְשָׁה (for ᵑ0 תחתים חָדְשִׁי, see חָדְשִׁי , קֵדֶשׁ); מַלְכֵי הַחִתִּים 1Kgs 10:29 = 2Chr 1:17 (both + מַלְכֵי אֲרָם), 2Kgs 7:6 (+ מַלְכֵי מִצְרַיִם, both feared by אֲרָם ). Hence it appears that הַחִתִּי (םׅ had their proper seat in the north — where also they were encountered by Assyrians from time of Tiglath-Pileser I (see Schr Dl1. c.), and by Egyptians from time of Tutmes III (see W Max Müll.l.c.) — (compare also Judg 3:3; Josh 11:3 above), but that individual Hittites were known in Israel (compare, besides 1Sam 2:6; 2Sam 11:3 etc., 1Kgs 9:20 above); that the Hittites were regarded (by J E D ) as one of the peoples of Canaan, and that the name even came to be used in more general sense for Canaanites. Only in P do they appear as having a definite settlement in the south, and are designated by בְּנֵיחֵֿת (see חֵת ), as well as by the adjective הַחִתִּי.
