Biblical Hebrew

I. צָרַר verb bind, tie up, be restricted, narrow, scant, cramped (Late Hebrew id.; Arabic bind, tie up; so Aramaic צְרַר, ); —

A.
transitive:
Qal Perfect 3 masculine singular צָרַר Hos 4:19; Prov 30:4 Imperative צוֺר Isa 8:16 Infinitive construct צְרוֺר Prov 26:8 (?); Participle active צֹרֵר Job 26:8 passive צָרוּר Hos 13:12, feminine צְרוּרָה 1Sam 25:29 feminine plural צְרֻרֹת Exod 12:34; 2Sam 20:3 — bind or tie up, of kneading-troughs Exod 12:34 ( E ); figurative of a life preserved by ׳ י, נֶפֶשׁ צְרוּרָה בִּצְרוֺר הַחַיִּים 1Sam 25:29 of preserving prophetic teaching Isa 8:16 of retention of guilt Hos 13:12 of ׳ י's binding waters in (a garment of) cloud Prov 30:4; Job 26:8 = shut up 2Sam 20:3 — צָרַר רוּחַ אֹתָהּ בִּכְנָפֶיהָ Hos 4:19 (si vera lectio) pregnantly, the wind hath wrapped her up in its wings, to carry her off; כִּצְרוֺר אֶבֶן Prov 26:8 like the tying up of a stone, but unintellig. in context (see Toy).
Pu`al Participle plural מְצֹרָרִים Josh 9:4 tied up (mended by tying), of old wine-skins.
