BDB3176 [H2752 H2753]

Biblical Hebrew

II. חֹרִי, חוֺרִי adjective, usually proper name [of a people or person] (probably = cave-dweller, so Thes Rob Ges MV VB ; see also W Max MüllAsien u. Europa 136, 155, 156) —

1. adjective [of a people] ׳ שֵׂעִיר הַח Gen 36:20 Seir the Horite ( P ); read ׳ הַחִ also Gen 36:2 for ᵑ0 הַחִוִּי
2. proper name [of a people] always with article; usually singular collective הַחֹרִיִ, ancient inhabitants of land of Edom Gen 14:6 (בְּהַרֲרָם שֵׂעִיר); in P called בְּנֵי שֵׂעִיר בְּאֶרֶץ אֱדוֺם Gen 36:21 (see v Gen 36:20); compare Gen 36:29 Gen 36:30 according to Deut 2:12 (where alone הַחֹרִים), Deut 2:22 they were driven out by sons of Esau (yet see Gen 36:1 c. and Di); 𝔊 ὁ Ξορραιος, οἱ Ξορραιοι, (Gen 36:29; Gen 36:30 Ξορρ(ε)ι).
3. proper name, masculine [person]
a. חֹרִי an Edomite Gen 36:22 = 1Chr 1:39 𝔊 Ξορρ(ε)ι.
b. חִוֺרי a Simeonite Num 13:5, but 𝔊 Σουρ(ε)ι, 𝔊 L Σουδρι. — I. חֹרִי see below I. חור.
