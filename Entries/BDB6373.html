Biblical Hebrew

[עָרָה] verb be naked, bare (Arabic be naked; Phoenician ערה [Pi`el] lay bare (compare Bloch Lzb); Assyrian ûru , nakedness, also desert waste (compare [ עָרָה] below) JägerBAS ii. 282 and references; Late Hebrew עֶרְוָה = Biblical Hebrew, ᵑ7 עִירְיְתָא; Syriac in Lexicons); —

Pi`el Perfect 3 masculine singular עֵרָה Isa 22:6; Zeph 2:14 Imperfect 3 masculine singular יְעָרֶה Isa 3:17 3 feminine singular וַהְּעַר Gen 24:20 2 masculine singular jussive תְּעַר Ps 141:8 3 masculine plural וִיעָרוּ 2Chr 24:11 Imperative masculine plural עָ֫רוּ Ps 137:7 (twice in verse); Infinitive absolute עָרוֺת Hab 3:13 (Ges«GKC:75n»§ 75n); —
1. lay bare Isa 3:17; Isa 22:6; Zeph 2:14 (indefinite subject); of laying foundations bare, i.e. tearing down walls, etc. Hab 3:13 (yet on text see Now), absolute עָ֫רוּ עָ֑רוּ Ps 137:7 (twice in verse).
2. lay bare by removing contents, empty, water-jar Gen 24:20 ( J ; + אֶל location), chest 2Chr 24:11.
3. pour out, אַלתְּֿעַר נַפְּשִׁי Ps 141:8 (i.e. slay).
Hiph`il Perfect 3 masculine singular ׃ הֶעֱרָה
1. make naked, of sexual offences Lev 20:18; Lev 20:19 (both || גִּלָּה).
2. pour out, לַמָּוֶת ׳ הע, נַפְשׁוֺ Isa 53:12 (figurative for slay, compare Qal 3).
Niph`al passive of Hiph`il 2: 3 masculine singular יֵעָרֶה עָלֵינוּ רוּחַ Isa 32:15 (figurative) until there be poured upon us a spirit from on high.
