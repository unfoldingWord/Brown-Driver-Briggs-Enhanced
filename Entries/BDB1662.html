Biblical Hebrew

II. גִּלְגָּל proper name [of a location] (= (sacred) circle of stones; compare Di on Josh 5:9, where word-play on roll away, off) — with article except Josh 5:9; Josh 12:23 —

1. place east of Jericho, where Israel lay encamped Josh 4:19; Josh 4:20; Josh 5:9; Josh 5:10; Josh 9:6; Josh 10:7; Josh 10:9; Josh 14:6 compare also Mic 6:5 & Judg 3:19 הַפְּסִילִים ׳ אֲשֶׁר אֶתהַֿגּ;? modern Tell Jeljul BdPal 169; perhaps also the place where sacrifices were offered 1Sam 10:8; 1Sam 11:14; 1Sam 11:15 (twice in verse); 1Sam 13:4; 1Sam 13:7 ; 1Sam 13:8; 1Sam 13:12; 1Sam 13:15 (in 1Sam 13:15 insert also with other words, Dr compare We ) 1Sam 15:12 ; 1Sam 15:21 ; 1Sam 15:33 place of illicit sacrifice Hos 4:15; Hos 9:15; Hos 12:12; Amos 4:4; Amos 5:5 (twice in verse), named also Judg 2:1; 1Sam 7:16; 2Sam 19:16; 2Sam 19:41 ? = בֵּית הַגִּלְגָּל Neh 12:29.
2. place on border between Judah & Benjamin Josh 15:17 (= גְּלִילוֺת Josh 18:17 compare Di).
3. place in Northern Israel, dwelling of prophets 2Kgs 2:1; 2Kgs 4:38, modern Jiljilie south from Nâbulus, southwest from Seilûn (Shiloh) compare Di RiHWB 518.
4. place in Northern Israel near Mt. Gerizim and Mt. Ebal Deut 11:30, identification with 3 by Ke RiHWB 518 SchenkelBL SS; but compare Di.
5. in phrase מֶלֶךְ גּוֺיִם לְגִּלְגָּל Josh 12:23, in list of Canaanitish kings; probably name of a (northern) district compare Di ( B βασιλέα Γεεὶ τῆς Γαλιλαίας — but 𝔊 L & Manuscripts Γοειμ τῆς Γελγελ); Di compare Isa 8:23.
