Biblical Hebrew

אַכְשָׁף proper name [of a location] in (Northern) Canaan, with a king Josh 11:1; Josh 12:20 situated on the border of Asher Josh 19:25, Egyptian 'A-k-sap WMMAs.u.Eur.181; 𝔊 B Αζειφ A Ἀχσαφ, 𝔊 L (Ἀ) χασαφ, etc.; site dubious; modern Iksâf or Kesâf, RobBR iii. 55, approximately 17 miles east of Tyre, and nearly 3 miles southwest of the great bend of the Litâny, is phonetically suitable, but much too far northeast for Josh 19:25 possibly there were two Akšaphs; compare Di Josh 11:1 BuhlGeogr.237. See further KrallTyrus U. Sidon 10, LagOnom. 218. 91, 3; 2d ed. 239.
