BDB166 [H178]

Biblical Hebrew

אוֺב noun masculine Job 32:19 skin-bottle, necromancer, etc. — absolute ׳ א Lev 20:27 + 8 t.; plural אֹבוֺת Lev 19:31 + 7 t. —

1. skin-bottle, only plural אֹבוֺת חֲדָשִׁים new (wine-) skins Job 32:19.
2. necromancer, in phrase אוֺב אוֺ יִדְּעֹנִי necromancer or wizard Lev 20:27 ( H ; usually translated 'a man also or woman that hath a familiar spirit or that is a wizard' RV ; but better a man or a woman, if there should be among them, a necromancer or wizard ; no sufficient reason for exceptional use of phrase here); וְיִדְּעֹנִי ׳ א Deut 18:11; 2Chr 33:6 = 2Kgs 21:6 (where וידענים ׳ א); הָאֹבוֺת וְהַיִּדְּעֹנִים Lev 19:31; Lev 20:6 ( H ) 1Sam 28:3 ; 1Sam 28:9 ; 2Kgs 23:24; Isa 8:19 (where repres. as chirping & muttering, in practice of their art of seeking dead for instruction, probably ventriloquism, & so 𝔊 ) Isa 19:3 .
3. ghost, Isa 29:4 וְהָיָה כְּאוֺב מֵאֶרֶץ קוֺלֵח וּמֵעָפָר אִמְרָתֵח תְּצַפְצֵף and thy voice shall be as a ghost from the ground and from the dust thy speech shall chirp (so Ge MV Ew De Che and others, but chirping might be of necromancer, as Isa 8:19).
4. necromancy אֵשֶׁת בַּעֲלַתאֿוֺב a woman who was mistress of necromancy 1Sam 28:7 (twice in verse); (> RSJPh xiv, 127 f makes אוֺב primarily a subterranean spirit, and signifies 2 only an abbreviation of ׳ בעלתא etc.); קסם בּאוֺב divine by necromancy 1Sam 28:8, which seems to be interpretation of 1Chr 10:13 ׳ שׁאל בא inquire by necromancy. (In these three examples אוֺב is usually interpreted as ghost or familiar spirit conceived as dwelling in necromancer; but this apparently not the ancient conception.)
