BDB5176 [H5203]

Biblical Hebrew

נָטַשׁ verb leave, forsake, permit ( ᵑ7 forsake; draw off skin of dead animal; Arabic = abstinens ab impuro according to †am (Frey)); —

Qal Perfect 3 masculine singular ׳ נ 1Sam 10:2, suffix 1 plural נְטָשָׁ֫נוּ Judg 6:13 2 masculine singular נָטַשְׁתָּ 1Sam 17:28, נָטַשְׁתָּה Isa 2:6, suffix נְטַשְׁתַּ֫נִי Gen 31:28, וּנְטַשְׁתָּהּ consecutive Exod 23:11, etc.; Imperfect יִטּשׁ 1Sam 12:22; Ps 94:14, יִטּוֺשׁ Hos 12:15, suffix 1 plural יִטְּשֵׁנוּ 1Kgs 8:57 1 plural נִטּשׁ Neh 10:32, etc.; Imperative נְטוֺשׁ Prov 17:14 Passive participle feminine נְטוּשָׁה Isa 21:15 masculine plural נְטֻשִׁים 1Sam 30:16 —
1. leave, let alone Exod 23:11 ( E ; = let field lie fallow, || שָׁמַט), compare וְנִטּשׁ אֶתהַֿשָּׁנָה הַשְּׁכִיעִיתּ וּמַשָּׁא כָליָֿ֑ד Neh 10:32 (i.e. leave field fallow and debts unclaimed); object quails, let them lie, (עַל location) Num 11:31 ( J E ), of Amalekites נְטֻשִׁים עַלפְּֿנֵי פָלהָֿאָרֶץ 1Sam 30:16 left to themselves over all the land; וְדָמָיו עָלָיו יִטּוֺשׁ Hos 12:15 and his mortal guilt shall he leave upon him; leave in charge of, entrust to (עַל person): sheep 1Sam 17:20; 1Sam 17:28 baggage 1Sam 17:22.
2. usually forsake, abandon, דִּבְרֵי הָאֲחֹנוֺת ׳ נ 1Sam 10:2 thy father hath abandoned the matter of the asses (ceased to be concerned about them); הָרִיב נְטוֺשׁ Prov 17:14 abandon contention; often subject ׳ י with accusative of person Judg 6:13; Isa 2:6; 1Sam 12:22; 1Kgs 8:57 (|| עָזַב) 2Kgs 21:14; Jer 7:29; Jer 12:7 (|| עָזַב), Jer 23:33; Jer 23:39; Ps 27:9 (|| עָזַב), Ps 94:14 (|| id.); of ׳ י leaving Phar. forsaken (on ground, compare Niph`al 1) Ezek 29:5 (+ ה locative), Ezek 32:4 (+ ב location); abandoning the sanctuary at Shiloh Ps 78:60 of men forsaking God Deut 32:15 (poem), Jer 15:6 compare אַלחִֿטּשׁ תּוֺרַת אִמֶּ֑ךָ Prov 1:8; Prov 6:20 of men abandoning king (under figure of cedar) Ezek 31:12 (twice in verse).
3. permit, only וְלֹא נְטַשְׁתַּנִי לְנַשֵּׁק ׳ לְבָנַי וגו Gen 31:28 ( E ) and hast not permitted me to kiss my sons, etc. — For וַתִּטּשׁ הַמִּלְחָמָה 1Sam 4:2 read probably וַתֵּט ( 𝔊 ἔκλινεν) Dr Klo Bu, or < (קשׁה) וַתִּקֶשׁ grew fierce HPS (Löhr proposes תִּנָּטֵשׁ compare Judg 15:9 etc.). For חֶרֶב נְטוּטָה Isa 21:15 (|| קֶשֶׁת דְּרוּכָה) read perhaps לְטוּשָׁה ׳ ח.
Niph`al Perfect 3 feminine singular נִטְּשָׁה Amos 5:2 3 plural נִטְּשׁוּ Isa 16:8 ; Isa 33:23 Imperfect 3 masculine plural וַיִּנָָּֽטְשׁוּ Judg 15:9 + 2 t.; —
1. be forsaken, Amos 5:2 she (Israel) lieth forsaken (עַל location).
2. be loosened or loose (properly be let alone , left to hang down) of ship's tackle Isa 33:23.
3. be let go, spread abroad, of warriors, Judg 15:19; 2Sam 5:18; 2Sam 5:22 (all with ב location, for purpose of plunder); of tendrils of vine Isa 16:8 (figurative of Moab).
Pu`al Perfect only אַדְמוֺן נֻטָּ֑שׁ Isa 32:14 palaces are abandoned, deserted (|| הֲמוֺן עִיר עֻזָּ֑ב ).
