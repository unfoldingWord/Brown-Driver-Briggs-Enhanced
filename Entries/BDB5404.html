Biblical Hebrew

II. נָשָׁא verb only Niph`al, Hiph`il beguile, deceive; — (kindred with I. שׁוא, from which, however, the forms can hardly be derived (conjectures Wecompare 2. 351)); —

Niph`al Perfect 3 plural נִשְּׁאוּ Isa 19:13 the princes have been beguiled (|| נוֺאֲלוּ, הִתְעוּ ).
Hiph`il Perfect הִשִּׁיא Jer 49:16, suffix הִשִּׁאַ֫נִי Gen 3:13 , הִשִּׁיאֶ֑ךָ Obad 1:3 2 masculine singular הִשֵּׁאתָ Jer 4:10 3 plural suffix הִשִּׁיא֫וּךָ Obad 1:7 Imperfect only jussive יַשִּׁיא 2Chr 32:15, so Ps 55:16 Qr (see below); יַשִּׁא 2Kgs 18:29; Isa 36:14, suffix יַשִּׁאֲךָ 2Kgs 19:10; Isa 37:10 2 masculine plural תַּשִּׁאוּ Jer 37:9 Infinitive absolute הַשֵּׁא Jer 4:10 — beguile, usually with accusative of person Gen 3:13 ( J ; not elsewhere Hexateuch); Jer 49:16; אַליַֿשִּׁאֲךָ 2Kgs 19:10 let not thy God beguile thee = Isa 37:10, of Hezekiah 2Chr 32:15 (compare below), אַלתַּֿשִּׁאוּ נַפְשֹׁתֵיכֶם Jer 37:9 do not deceive yourselves; also followed by ל person אַליַֿשִּׁא לָכֶם 2Kgs 18:29 = Isa 36:14 (= 2Chr 32:15, see above), Jer 29:8 הַזֶּה הַשֵּׁא הִשֵּׁאתָ לָעָם Jer 4:10 thou hast utterly beguiled this people; ישׁימות עָלֵימוֺ Ps 55:16 Kt apparently = desolations (be) upon them! (but elsewhere only in proper name, of a location, compare p. 445 above), < Qr יַשִּׁיא מָוֶת עלימו Ew Ol Pe De NowHup, compare 𝔊 , let death (beguile them, i.e.) come deceitfully upon them!
