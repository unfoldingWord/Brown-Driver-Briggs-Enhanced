BDB7756 [H7281]

Biblical Hebrew

רֶ֫גַע noun masculine a moment (Late Hebrew id.; ᵑ7 ריגעא Eccl 9:12: probably properly a movement, i.e. twinkling, of the eye; compare momentum, i.e. movimentum); — absolute ׳ ר, רָ֑נַע Num 16:21 +, plural רְגָעִים; —

a. Ps 30:6 בְּאַפּוֺ ׳ ר a moment (passes) in his anger (opposed to חיים ברצונו); Isa 54:7 קָטֹן ׳ בְּר during a little moment
b. usually in adverb phrases: — (a) as adverb accusative, (α) Exod 33:5 אֶהָד ׳ ר for one moment, so ׳ ר alone Isa 54:8 repeated Jer 18:7; Jer 18:9 רֶגַע ֗֗֗ רֶגַע at another moment; . . . at another moment; (β) in a moment, suddenly, Ps 6:11 יָשׁוּבוּ יֵבשׁוּ רָ֑גַע, Jer 4:20; Isa 47:9; Job 34:20. (b) בְּרֶגַע in a moment Job 21:13 שְׁאוֺל יַחַ֫תּוּ ׳ ובר (of a quick and painless death; but see Job 34:20; Ps 73:19: Hoffm Buhl Baer Bu Du [compare 𝔊 ἐν ἀναπαύσει, ᵑ7 בְּמַרְגּוֺעָא] take רֶגַע (or read רְגֹעַ) as substantive [√ II. רָגַע] in tranquility, of a peaceful death). So (c) ׳ כְּר Num 16:21 = Num 17:10 וַאֲכַלֶּה אֶתֵכֶם כְּרָ֑גַע, Ps 73:19. (d) ׳ כְּמוֺ ר Lam 4:6. (e ) עֲדֵי רָ֑גַע while a moment lasts Job 20:5. (f) ׳ כִּמְעַט ר like the littleness of a moment = for a little moment Isa 26:20; Ezra 9:8. ( g) plural לִרְגָעִים by moments, at every moment, Isa 27:8; Ezek 26:16 ׳ וְחָֽרְדוּ ל (so Ezek 32:10), Job 7:18 (|| לִבְקָרִים).
