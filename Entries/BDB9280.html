Biblical Aramaic

אַזְדָּא (so Gi, with most MSS .; Baer אָֽזְדָא adjective sure, assured (Persian azda , Sanskrit addhâ , 'certain' (KernZDMG 1869, 220); so first NöKAT 2 617; confirmed now by Egyptian Aramaic (RÉS361, B 3) הן אזד יחעבד מן if it is made certain (certified) by, etc.; > 𝔊 Ki AV and others (see Thea) as verb is gone, or (אָֽזְדָא) is going, from Talmud אֲזַד (= אֲזַל) go away (LevyNHWB i. 50); so ScheftMGWJ 1903, 310 from Zend * azda 'gone,' participle passive of azaiti 'go'); — Dan 2:5 מִלְּתָה מִנִּי אַוְדָּא, Dan 2:8 דִּיאַֿזְדָּא מִנִּי מִלְּתָא the word is assured on my part (the thing is fully resolved upon by me: compareSyrver ; IE קַיָּימָא וֶאְמֶת); + Dan 3:14(see צְדָא ).
